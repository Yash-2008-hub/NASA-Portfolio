Hubble Space Telescope

Launched: April 24, 1990

Mission Overview

The Hubble Space Telescope is a large space telescope that was launched into low Earth orbit in 1990 and remains in operation. It was not the first space telescope, but it is one of the largest and most versatile, well known both as a vital research tool and as a public relations boon for astronomy.

Key Features

  • Primary mirror diameter: 2.4 meters (7.9 ft)
  • Orbit: 547 kilometers (340 mi) above Earth
  • Weight: 11,110 kg (24,490 lb)
  • Length: 13.2 m (43.5 ft)

Major Discoveries

  • Determined the rate of expansion of the universe
  • Discovered dark energy
  • Observed the formation of stars and planets
  • Captured detailed images of distant galaxies
  • Studied black holes and their effects

Scientific Impact

Hubble has made more than 1.5 million observations since its mission began in 1990. Astronomers using Hubble data have published more than 18,000 scientific papers, making it one of the most productive scientific instruments ever built.
