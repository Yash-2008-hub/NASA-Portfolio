Mars Exploration Program

Ongoing since 2000

Program Overview

The Mars Exploration Program is a long-term effort to explore the planet Mars, funded and led by NASA. The program includes a series of robotic orbiters, landers, and rovers that have been studying the Red Planet since 2000.

Key Missions

  • Spirit and Opportunity (2004-2018)
  • Curiosity (2012-present)
  • Perseverance (2021-present)
  • Ingenuity Helicopter (2021-present)

Major Discoveries

  • Evidence of past water on Mars
  • Ancient riverbeds and lakebeds
  • Organic molecules in Martian soil
  • Seasonal methane variations
  • Evidence of ancient microbial life

Future Goals

The program's goals include determining if life ever arose on Mars, characterizing the climate of Mars, characterizing the geology of Mars, and preparing for human exploration. The program is also developing the technology needed for a human mission to Mars.
